THE HISTORY
1865 – 1950 Nearly 6,500 Black people were lynched by white mobs in America. These were public events — attended by crowds, photographed, celebrated. Law enforcement rarely intervened. No one was prosecuted.
Tulsa, 1921 The Greenwood District — Black Wall Street — was destroyed by a white mob over 18 hours. Up to 300 Black residents killed. 10,000 left homeless. 35 blocks burned. No criminal charges were ever filed.
Rosewood, 1923 A prosperous Black town in Florida was burned to the ground by a white mob. At least 6 Black residents killed, possibly dozens more. The town was wiped from the map. The state did not acknowledge it for 70 years.
Osage Nation, 1920s After oil was discovered on Osage land, white men systematically murdered Osage people to steal their mineral rights. At least 60 confirmed killings. The FBI's first major case. Most perpetrators went free.
Still today 2024 was the deadliest year for police killings since records began. Black Americans are killed by police at nearly 3 times the rate of white Americans. Less than 2% of officers involved are ever charged.
